Planning a Student Party at CERN

This webpage will try to explain what you have to think about when hosting a large party for the students at CERN.

Finding a Theme and a Date

The best parties are the parties with a good theme. Since people at CERN are interested in physics, a physics-related theme is never a bad idea. Be careful with picking a theme which requires too much of the participants. If you plan a party in the weekend (Friday and Saturday), it is likely that many people will be away from CERN, so consider planning something on a Thursday.

The Summer Student Fund

Over the years, summer students have accumulated the profit from parties into a summer student fund. The money in this fund goes towards buying new equipment for the Pump Room and other student related purposes (for example summer student t-shirts). Currently, David Kofoed Wind is responsible for the student fund. Remember, that all the money in the summer student fund comes from students, so if we waste it, it is gone!

Booking a Room

For large parties at CERN, the Pump Room/Salle polyvale (216-R-401) is the only place to be. The Pump Room can be booked at this website. The Pump Room can hold 200 participants which should be fine for most parties. When booking the room for a party, it can be a good idea to book it until midnight and again from midnight to noon the next day to give more time for cleaning. There is a 200 CHF deposit for booking the Pump Room. Another place to have a smaller party is the barbecue area at Building 9405.

Signing the Forms

When you book a room for a large party, a series of people have to sign a form indicating who is responsible for the party. The people who need to sign the form is the organizer (you), your supervisor, somebody in the main administration and the people handling the room bookings. You will get told who should sign the papers, and there should be no problems with getting signatures.

The Bar

At self-organized parties, no alcohol above 15% can be served, and all distribution of alcohol must be supervised by someone. There should always be non-alcoholic drinks available in the bar. A good place to buy supplies for the bar is Intermarche, so borrow a car from a good friend. At the last party I helped organize (with around 200 participants), around 830 beers and 20 litres of alcohol was consumed. We took 2CHF per beer, 3CHF for an alcoholic drink and 1CHF for a non-alcoholic drink. A good argument for charging more for drinks is that less people get really drunk, which means there is less people getting into trouble which you are responsible for!

Branding and PR

If you want to host a party for all the students, the Facebook groups are good place to start. There is a Facebook group for the summer students and a Facebook group for young people at CERN. It is also possible to borrow the microphone between the lectures and tell about your party.

Entrance Fee

It is possible to take a small entrance fee of a couple of francs. Charging an entrance fee make the party more formal, and helps ensure that you don't loose money on the party.

Cleaning

When booking the Pump Room, there is a cleaning fee of 70CHF which goes toward a professional cleaning team washing the floor on the day after the party. It is still your own responsibility to clean the room, the outside and remove puke and other things left behind. It can be a good idea to take the worst part of the cleaning immediatly after the party, and only leave a small bit for the next morning.

Music

Of course music is an important part of any good party. Consider finding a DJ, for example one you know or make a post on the Facebook groups. Unless you have a very specific theme, you will most likely want a DJ who plays general top 40 hits. Consider paying the DJ a small amount of the profits, or at least make sure he/she has everything needed (red bulls, a fan or other things). If music-equipment is missing, talk to the people responsible for the student fund to purchase new equipment.
